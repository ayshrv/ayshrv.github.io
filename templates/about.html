About Me

I am a final-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, advised by Andrew Owens. I am broadly interested in motion understanding, image and video generation, and multimodal learning. My research focuses on self-supervised learning for visual correspondence, with an emphasis on leveraging priors from large-scale video models and building scalable visual representations that transfer across tasks and modalities. More recently, I have been working on improving the efficiency of video diffusion models. I am a recipient of the PAMI Mark Everingham Prize Career Award (2025).

I completed my M.S. in Computer Science at Georgia Tech, advised by Devi Parikh. Prior to that, I was a visiting research scholar at Georgia Tech, working with Devi Parikh and Dhruv Batra.

I completed my undergraduate degree in Computer Science at the Indian Institute of Technology (BHU) Varanasi.
